Exercise 11.

Consider a general balanced transportation problem with \(w_1, \ldots, w_n\) warehouses, \(m_1, \ldots, m_m\) markets, and cost to ship from warehouse \(i\) to market \(j\) is \(c_{ij}\text{.}\)
(a)
Write out a canonical minimization problem which models this problem (it’s ok to use inequalities here that may not make complete sense, so long as they aren’t strict inequalities)
(b)
State the dual problem to the transportation problem.
(c)
Prove that in Definition 6.2.1, for a valid choice of \(a_i, b_j\) in step 2 that
\begin{equation*} f(\x) = \displaystyle \sum_{i} a_iw_i+\sum_{j} b_jm_j \end{equation*}
How does this relate to the dual problem to the transportation problem?
(d)
Consider the non-negativity condition in step 4 of Definition 6.2.1. How does this relate to the constraints of the dual problem to the transportation problem.
