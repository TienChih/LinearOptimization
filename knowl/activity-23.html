Activity 2.3.7.

We model the shipping problem in Exploration 2.3.6 as a linear optimization problem. Let \(x_{ij}\) denote the quantity in tons of goods shipped from \(v_i\) to \(v_j\text{.}\)

(a)

What is the objective function of this problem?
  1. \(f(\x)=\displaystyle \sum_{i, j}x_{ij}\text{.}\)
  2. \(f(\x)=\displaystyle \sum_{i, j}x_{ij}c_{ij}\text{.}\)
  3. \(f(\x)=\displaystyle \sum_{i, j}x_{ij}w_{ij}\text{.}\)
  4. \(f(\x)=\displaystyle \sum_{j}x_{0j}\text{.}\)
  5. \(f(\x)=\displaystyle \sum_{j}x_{0j}c_{0j}\text{.}\)
  6. \(f(\x)=\displaystyle \sum_{j}x_{0j}w_{0j}\text{.}\)

(b)

For each \(ij\) edge, there is a constraint for the shipping capacity of that edge. What (in)equality represents that capacity?
  1. \(x_{ij}\leq w_{ij}\text{.}\)
  2. \(x_{ij}\geq w_{ij}\text{.}\)
  3. \(x_{ij}= w_{ij}\text{.}\)
  4. \(x_{ij}\leq c_{ij}\text{.}\)
  5. \(x_{ij}\geq c_{ij}\text{.}\)
  6. \(x_{ij}= c_{ij}\text{.}\)

(c)

For each vertex \(v_k\) excluding the source and sink, what (in)equality represents the conservation of flow?
  1. \(\displaystyle \sum_{i}x_{ik}-\sum_{j}x_{kj}\leq 0\text{.}\)
  2. \(\displaystyle \sum_{i}x_{ik}-\sum_{j}x_{kj}\geq 0\text{.}\)
  3. \(\displaystyle \sum_{i}x_{ik}-\sum_{j}x_{kj} = 0\text{.}\)

(d)

Let \(F\) be the total amount of goods to be shipped, in this case \(F=10\text{.}\) What equality represents this constraint?
  1. \(\displaystyle \sum_{i, j}x_{ij}c_{ij}= F\text{.}\)
  2. \(\displaystyle \sum_{i, j}x_{ij}w_{ij}= F\text{.}\)
  3. \(\displaystyle \sum_{i, j}x_{ij}=F\text{.}\)
  4. \(\displaystyle \sum_{j}x_{0j}= F\text{.}\)
  5. \(\displaystyle \sum_{j}x_{0j}c_{0j}=10\text{.}\)
  6. \(\displaystyle \sum_{j}x_{0j}w_{0j}=10\text{.}\)

(e)

Write out the linear optimization problem modeling Exploration 2.3.6 and solve it.
