Activity 5.2.2.

We prove an interesting way to think of the optimal strategies.
Let \(\q'\) denote a fixed column strategy, and let \(v=\displaystyle \max_{1\leq i\leq n}A_{(i)}\q'\text{,}\) and let \(u=\displaystyle\max_{\p \in F_{\p} } \p^\top A\q\)

(a)

Recall that \(\p^\top A\q' = \displaystyle \sum_{i=1}^n \p_i A_{(i)}\q'\text{.}\) Prove that \(\p^\top A\q' \leq v\text{.}\)

(b)

Why must \(u\leq v\text{?}\)

(c)

Show that there is a (very simple) row strategy \(\p''\) where \((\p'')^\top A \q'=v\text{.}\)

(d)

Why must \(v\leq u\text{?}\)

(e)

What have we proven?
