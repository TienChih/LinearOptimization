Exercise 6.

Let \(N\) be a capacitated network with a unique source and sink, with no edges going into the source or out of the sink.
(a)
Given an example for \(N\) such that \(N\) has non-unique max-flows.
(b)
Given an example for \(N\) such that \(N\) has non-unique min-cuts.
(c)
Let \(x_{ij}\) denote any max-flow for \(N\) with value \(f\) and \((V_1, V_2)\) denote any min-cut (not neccesarily produced by \(x_{ij}\) and Definition 1.2.4). Let \((V_1', V_2')\) be the cut generated \(x_{ij}\) via Definition 1.2.4.
Prove that \(x_{ij}=c_{ij}\) for any \(v_i\in V_1, v_j=V_2\text{.}\)
(d)
Let \(x_{ij}, x'_{ij}\) be two distinct max-flows on \(N\text{,}\) and \((V_1, V_2), (V'_1, V'_2)\) be the cuts produced by Definition 1.2.4 on with these flows respectively. Prove that \((V_1, V_2) = (V'_1, V'_2)\text{.}\)
