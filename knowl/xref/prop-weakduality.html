Proposition 4.2.2.

For a primal maximization linear programming problem with objective function \(f\) and dual objective \(g\text{,}\) we have that
\begin{equation*} f\leq g \end{equation*}
In particular, if there is a feasible primal solution \(\x'\) and feasible dual solution \(\y'\) such that
\begin{equation*} f(\x')=g(\y') \end{equation*}
then \(\x', \y'\) are optimal solutions for the primal and dual problems respectively.
