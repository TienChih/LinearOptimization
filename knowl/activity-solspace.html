Activity 1.1.2.

Consider the augemnted matrix
\begin{equation*} M=\left(\begin{array}{cccc|c} a_{11} & a_{12} & \cdots & a_{1n} & b_1 \\ a_{21} & a_{22} & \cdots & a_{2n}& b_2 \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots & \vdots \\ a_{m1} & a_{m2} & \cdots & a_{mn} & b_m \\ \end{array}\right) = (A|b). \end{equation*}

(a)

What given a fixed \(n\text{,}\) what should \(m\) be for the system of equations encoded by \(M\) to have a unique solution?

(b)

What does this mean geometrically?

(c)

If the rows of \(A\) are independent, and \(m\lt n\text{,}\) then what is the dimension of the solution space of \(M\text{?}\)
