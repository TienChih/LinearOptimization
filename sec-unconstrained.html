    Section 4.2 Super Constrained Variables

    Activity 4.2.1.

    Suppose we wanted to solve the linear program:
    \begin{align*} \textbf{Maximize: } f(\mathbf{x})= x+y+z \amp \\ \textbf{Subject to: } 2x+3z\amp \leq 2\\ 4x-y\amp \leq 1\\ 5y-2z\amp \leq 5\\ x, y, z\amp \geq 0 \end{align*}

    (a)

    Plot the feasible region, what dimension is it?
    Hint.

    (b)

    Suppose we added in the constraint \(3x+2y-z=8\) Plot the feasible region, what dimension is it?
    Hint.

    (c)

    Consider the inequality \(3x+2y-z\leq 8\) captured by the equality \(3x+2y-z - 8=-t_4\text{.}\) What value must \(t_4\) so that \(3x+2y-z\leq 8\) is always an equality? Call this value ?.

    (d)

    Note that this progam may be encoded in the tableau:
    \begin{equation*} \begin{array}{|ccc|c|c} x \amp y\amp z \amp -1 \amp \\ \hline 2\amp 0 \amp 3 \amp2\amp -t_1 \\ 4 \amp -1 \amp 0 \amp 1\amp -t_2 \\ 0 \amp 5 \amp -2 \amp 5\amp -t_3 \\ 3 \amp 2^* \amp -1 \amp 8\amp ? \\ \hline 1 \amp 1 \amp 1 \amp 0\amp f \\ \hline \end{array} \end{equation*}
    Without comuting the tableau, what point are we on if we pivot on the entry with a *? Is it feasible?

    (e)

    As we traverse corner points on the way to an optimal solution, would we ever leave the plane \(3x+2y-z=8\text{?}\)

    (f)

    After the last pivot, our tableau has the form:
    \begin{equation*} \begin{array}{|ccc|c|c} x \amp ? \amp z \amp -1 \amp \\ \hline a_{11}\amp a_{12} \amp a_{13} \amp b_1 \amp -t_1 \\ a_{21} \amp a_{22} \amp a_{23} \amp b_2\amp -t_2 \\ a_{31} \amp a_{32} \amp a_{33} \amp b_3\amp -t_3 \\ a_{41} \amp a_{42} \amp a_{43} \amp b_4\amp y \\ \hline c_1 \amp c_2 \amp c_3 \amp d\amp f \\ \hline \end{array} \end{equation*}
    Ignoring the specific values of the entries of this tableau, using the value for ? computed earlier, rewrite each of the above equations in terms of \(x, z\text{.}\) What information did the ? column provide?

    Observation 4.2.1.

    An equality constraint can be written as:
    \begin{equation*} a_{i1}x_1+a_{i2}x_2+\cdots+a_{im}x_m-b_i = 0 \end{equation*}
    and thus recorded in a tableau as:
    \begin{equation*} \begin{array}{|cccccc|c|c} x_1 \amp x_2 \amp \cdots \amp x_j\amp \cdots \amp x_m \amp -1\\ \hline a_{11} \amp a_{12}\amp \cdots \amp a_{1j}\amp \cdots \amp a_{1m}\amp b_1 \amp -t_1 \\ a_{21} \amp a_{22}\amp \cdots \amp a_{2j}\amp \cdots\amp a_{2m}\amp b_2 \amp -t_2 \\ \vdots \amp \vdots \amp \vdots \amp \vdots\amp \vdots \amp \vdots\amp \vdots \amp\vdots \\ a_{i1} \amp a_{i2}\amp \cdots \amp a_{ij}^*\amp \cdots \amp a_{im}\amp b_i \amp 0 \\ \vdots \amp \vdots \amp \ddots \amp \vdots\amp \cdots \amp \vdots\amp \vdots \amp\vdots \\ a_{n1} \amp a_{n2}\amp \cdots \amp a_{nj}\amp \cdots \amp a_{nm}\amp b_n \amp -t_n \\ \hline c_1 \amp c_2\amp \cdots \amp c_j \amp \cdots \amp c_m \amp d \amp = f \\ \hline \end{array} \end{equation*}
    If we pivot on the \(a_{ij}^*\) entry, we obtain:
    \begin{equation*} \begin{array}{|cccccc|c|c} x_1 \amp x_2 \amp \cdots \amp 0\amp \cdots \amp x_m \amp -1\\ \hline ? \amp ? \amp \cdots \amp ?\amp \cdots \amp ?\amp ? \amp -t_1 \\ ? \amp ? \amp \cdots \amp ?\amp \cdots \amp ?\amp ? \amp -t_2 \\ \vdots \amp \vdots \amp \vdots \amp \vdots\amp \vdots \amp \vdots\amp \vdots \amp\vdots \\ ? \amp ? \amp \cdots \amp ?\amp \cdots \amp ?\amp ? \amp x_j \\ \vdots \amp \vdots \amp \ddots \amp \vdots\amp \cdots \amp \vdots\amp \vdots \amp\vdots \\ ? \amp ? \amp \cdots \amp ?\amp \cdots \amp ?\amp ? \amp -t_n \\ \hline ? \amp ? \amp \cdots \amp ?\amp \cdots \amp ?\amp ? \amp = f \\ \hline \end{array} \end{equation*}
    Then depending on your perspective, we can either delete the 0 column because it does not contribute information algebraically, or because it is redundant geometrically, and we restrict ourselves to a \(m-1\) dimensional solution space. Either way, removing this column gives us:
    \begin{equation*} \begin{array}{|cccc|c|c} x_1 \amp x_2 \amp \cdots \amp x_m \amp -1\\ \hline ? \amp ? \amp \cdots \amp ?\amp ? \amp -t_1 \\ ? \amp ? \amp \cdots \amp ?\amp ? \amp -t_2 \\ \vdots \amp \vdots \amp \vdots \amp \vdots\amp \vdots \amp\vdots \\ ? \amp ? \amp \cdots \amp ?\amp ? \amp x_j \\ \vdots \amp \vdots \amp \ddots \amp \vdots\amp \vdots \amp\vdots \\ ? \amp ? \amp \cdots \amp ?\amp ? \amp -t_n \\ \hline ? \amp ? \amp \cdots \amp ?\amp ? \amp = f \\ \hline \end{array} \end{equation*}

    Activity 4.2.3.

    Consider the linear program:
    \begin{align*} \textbf{Maximize: } f(\mathbf{x})= 2x+y-2z \amp \\ \textbf{Subject to: } x+y+z\amp \leq 1\\ y+4z\amp = 2\\ x, y, z\amp \geq 0 \end{align*}

    (a)

    Record this program in a tableau with an equality constraint.
    \begin{equation*} \begin{array}{|ccc|c|c} x \amp y \amp z \amp -1\\ \hline ? \amp ? \amp ?\amp ? \amp -t_1 \\ ? \amp ? \amp ?\amp ? \amp 0 \\ \hline ? \amp ? \amp ?\amp ? \amp = f \\ \hline \end{array} \end{equation*}

    (b)

    Pivot on the entry with \(*\text{.}\)
    \begin{equation*} \begin{array}{|ccc|c|c} x \amp y \amp z \amp -1\\ \hline ? \amp ? \amp ?\amp ? \amp -t_1 \\ ? \amp ?^* \amp ?\amp ? \amp 0 \\ \hline ? \amp ? \amp ?\amp ? \amp = f \\ \hline \end{array} \end{equation*}

    (c)

    Pivot on the entry with \(*\text{.}\)
    \begin{equation*} \begin{array}{|ccc|c|c} x \amp 0 \amp z \amp -1\\ \hline ? \amp ? \amp ?\amp ? \amp -t_1 \\ ? \amp ? \amp ?\amp ? \amp y \\ \hline ? \amp ? \amp ?\amp ? \amp = f \\ \hline \end{array} \end{equation*}
    Rewrite the 3 rows as linear equalities, and verify that the 0 column contributes nothing.

    (d)

    Delete the 0 column and solve the remaining system.
